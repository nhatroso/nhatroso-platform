## Stakeholder Map

---

## 1. Primary Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role | Key Interests | Influence | Interest Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Landlord / Boarding-house manager** | Primary user and decision-maker | Room management, revenue tracking, utilities automation, dispute reduction | Very high | Very high |
| **Tenant** | End user | Transparent bills, fast payment, easy contract tracking | Medium | High |
| **Student project team** | Analysis, design, system development | Deliver on time, meet technical goals | Very high | Very high |
| **Academic supervisor** | Advisor and academic approval | Professional quality, methodology, capstone outcomes | High | High |

---

## 2. Secondary Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role | Interests | Influence | Interest Level |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Payment service / bank** | Processes QR transactions | Accuracy, security, reconciliation | Medium | Medium |
| **Infrastructure provider (cloud, server)** | Runs the system | Stability, performance, uptime | Medium | Low–Medium |
| **Potential landlord community** | Future users | Value, cost, ease of rollout | Low | Medium |

---

## 3. Power–Interest Matrix

**Manage closely (High Power – High Interest)**

- Landlord / manager
- Student project team
- Academic supervisor

→ **Engage frequently and obtain sign-off.**

**Keep satisfied (High Power – Low Interest)**

- Infrastructure provider

→ **Provide periodic updates and meet SLA commitments.**

**Keep informed (Low Power – High Interest)**

- Tenants

→ **Collect UX feedback and provide fast support.**

**Monitor (Low Power – Low Interest)**

- Potential user community

→ **Monitor for future expansion.**

---

## 4. Short conclusion

The most critical stakeholder group is the **landlord / boarding-house manager** because they **decide whether to adopt the system and receive direct business value**, while **tenants** primarily **determine the user experience and overall product acceptance**.
